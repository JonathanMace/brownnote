# Suggested reviewers for Paper 6 (JASA)

Suggested reviewers were chosen to cover whole-body vibration perception,
low-frequency acoustics, concert acoustics, vibroacoustics / structural
acoustics, and biomedical acoustics. All are outside Microsoft Research
and the University of Auckland. Before submission, the authors should
still screen for conflicts of interest, recent collaborations, and
editorial roles.

## 1. Neil J. Mansfield
- **Affiliation:** School of Design and Creative Arts, Loughborough University
- **Expertise:** Whole-body vibration perception, human biodynamic response, vibration comfort and health
- **Why a good fit:** Author of *Human Response to Vibration* (CRC Press, 2005), which is cited in the manuscript as a primary source for perception thresholds. He is well placed to judge the validity of the ISO 2631-derived perception model and the seat-to-abdomen transmissibility assumptions.
- **Source:** https://www.lboro.ac.uk/departments/design-school/staff/neil-mansfield/

## 2. Henrik Møller
- **Affiliation:** Department of Electronic Systems, Aalborg University
- **Expertise:** Low-frequency hearing, infrasound perception, equal-loudness contours, noise measurement
- **Why a good fit:** Co-author of the widely cited "Hearing at low and infrasonic frequencies" (Noise & Health, 2004), which anchors the manuscript's discussion of the psychoacoustic boundary between hearing and whole-body vibration in the sub-bass band. He would be a strong reviewer for the low-frequency acoustic coupling arguments and the airborne pathway's near-miss interpretation.
- **Source:** https://vbn.aau.dk/en/persons/acoustic

## 3. Neil P. McAngus Todd
- **Affiliation:** Department of Psychology, University of Manchester
- **Expertise:** Vestibular and somatosensory responses to music, bass perception, concert acoustics and physiology
- **Why a good fit:** His JASA paper on vestibular responses to loud dance music (Todd & Cody, 2000) is cited in the manuscript and directly addresses the physiological basis of bass perception at concert levels. He is uniquely positioned to evaluate whether the pathway-partition framework captures the full sensory experience, including the vestibular and somatosensory contributions discussed in the paper.
- **Source:** https://research.manchester.ac.uk/en/persons/neil.todd

## 4. Nicole Kessissoglou
- **Affiliation:** School of Mechanical and Manufacturing Engineering, UNSW Sydney
- **Expertise:** Structural acoustics, vibroacoustics, fluid--structure interaction, acoustic radiation from shells and plates
- **Why a good fit:** Extensive JASA publication record on acoustic scattering, vibration of fluid-loaded shells, and power-flow methods in coupled structural--acoustic systems. She is well matched to assess both the energy-consistent reciprocity formulation for airborne coupling and the damped-plate power-balance model for the floor transmission pathway.
- **Source:** https://www.unsw.edu.au/staff/nicole-kessissoglou

## 5. Thomas J. Royston
- **Affiliation:** Department of Biomedical Engineering, University of Illinois Chicago
- **Expertise:** Vibro-acoustics of biological tissues, tissue mechanical characterisation, elastography, wave propagation in soft media
- **Why a good fit:** Publishes at the intersection of acoustics and tissue biomechanics, including JASA work on mechanical excitation and wave propagation in viscoelastic biological media. He is an ideal reviewer for the viscoelastic oblate spheroidal shell model of the abdomen and the tissue-property assumptions (Young's modulus, loss tangent, frequency dependence) that underpin the displacement calculations.
- **Source:** https://bme.uic.edu/profiles/royston-thomas/
