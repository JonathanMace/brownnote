# Draft AI disclosure for JASA / AIP submission

## Recommended statement

During manuscript preparation, the authors used GitHub Copilot CLI (GitHub, Inc.) to assist with code drafting, literature organisation, and language editing. All equations, simulations, references, interpretations, and conclusions were reviewed and verified by the human authors, who take full responsibility for the content of the manuscript. No generative AI tool was used to create or alter original research data or figures.

## Placement note

AIP Publishing's current AI policy permits undisclosed use of AI for readability only, but requires disclosure in the **Methods** section when AI use could affect findings or conclusions (for example through code, analysis, or information synthesis). Because Copilot assistance in this project extended beyond copy-editing, the conservative JASA-compliant approach is to include a disclosure statement in the manuscript and submission metadata.

## Policy summary checked for this package

- AI used only for readability improvement: disclosure not required.
- AI that could affect findings or conclusions: disclose in **Methods** with the tool name, version, provider, and purpose.
- AI tools cannot be listed as authors.
- AI must not be used to fabricate references or alter original research data/images.

Source checked: https://publishing.aip.org/resources/researchers/policies-and-ethics/ai-policy/
