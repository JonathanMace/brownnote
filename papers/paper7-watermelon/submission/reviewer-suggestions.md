# Suggested reviewers for Paper 7

Suggested reviewers were chosen to cover postharvest quality assessment,
fruit acoustics, non-destructive testing, and shell mechanics applied to
biological structures. These are editorial suggestions only; the authors
should still screen for conflicts of interest, recent collaborations, and
other exclusions before submission.

## 1. Jordi Gené-Mola
- **Affiliation:** Research Group in AgroICT and Precision Agriculture (GRAP), Universitat de Lleida, Spain
- **Expertise:** Non-destructive fruit quality assessment, acoustic and vibrational methods, postharvest sensing
- **Why a good fit:** Active in acoustic-based fruit quality estimation and would be well placed to assess the practical relevance of a physics-based inversion for postharvest sorting.

## 2. Bart Nicolaï
- **Affiliation:** Division of Mechatronics, Biostatistics and Sensors (MeBioS), KU Leuven, Belgium
- **Expertise:** Postharvest biology, non-destructive quality measurement, multiscale modelling of fruit
- **Why a good fit:** A leading authority on postharvest quality modelling who has reviewed the intersection of physics-based and data-driven approaches to fruit assessment.

## 3. Manuela Zude-Sasse
- **Affiliation:** Leibniz Institute for Agricultural Engineering and Bioeconomy (ATB), Potsdam, Germany
- **Expertise:** Optical and acoustic sensing for fruit maturity, non-destructive quality assessment
- **Why a good fit:** Has published extensively on non-destructive fruit quality methods and would bring strong domain knowledge of the postharvest sensing landscape.

## 4. Naoshi Kondo
- **Affiliation:** Graduate School of Agriculture, Kyoto University, Japan
- **Expertise:** Agricultural robotics, acoustic sensing, non-destructive evaluation of fruits and vegetables
- **Why a good fit:** Well matched to the paper's aim of turning an acoustic measurement into a quantitative mechanical parameter for fruit grading.

## 5. Renfu Lu
- **Affiliation:** United States Department of Agriculture (USDA), Agricultural Research Service, East Lansing, MI
- **Expertise:** Non-destructive food quality evaluation, spectroscopy and acoustic methods for fruit firmness
- **Why a good fit:** Extensive experience in physics-based and sensor-based approaches to fruit firmness estimation; would bring a rigorous applied-science perspective.

## 6. Marco Amabili
- **Affiliation:** Department of Mechanical Engineering, McGill University, Canada
- **Expertise:** Shell vibration, fluid–structure interaction, nonlinear shell mechanics
- **Why a good fit:** Strong match to the paper's equivalent-sphere shell modelling and could assess the structural-mechanics underpinnings of the watermelon model.
