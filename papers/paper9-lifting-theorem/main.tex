\title{Geometry-dependent identifiability in spectral inverse problems: oblate lifting without prolate improvement}
\author{Jonathan Mace\\Microsoft Research \and Brian R.\ Mace\\University of Auckland}
\date{}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We consider the spectral inverse problem for fluid-loaded elastic shells using
the five-mode flexural map $\mathbf{f}=[f_2,\ldots,f_6]^\mathsf{T}$.  The
equivalent-sphere reduction is rank deficient ($\sigma_3=0$), and at the exact
sphere this rank deficiency persists because spherical symmetry forces the two
geometric Jacobian columns to be proportional.  The positive $\sigma_3$ returned
by a finite Ritz computation at $a=c$ is a discretisation artefact that grows
with basis size; the correct near-sphere expansion is
$\sigma_3(\varepsilon)=\lambda_1\varepsilon^2+O(\varepsilon^4)$ with
$\lambda_1>0$.  The oblate branch therefore shows quadratic growth in the
weakest sensitivity direction, reaching
$\kappa_\mathrm{oblate}=\num{69.4}$ at the canonical geometry, whereas a
prolate continuation at $c/a=\num{1.5}$ gives $\kappa_\mathrm{prolate}=156$
with no systematic improvement across the tested range.  The observed
conditioning improvement is consistent with mode-dependent curvature sampling,
though a full mechanistic analysis is beyond the scope of this short
communication.
\end{abstract}
