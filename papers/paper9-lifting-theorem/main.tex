\title{Geometry-dependent identifiability in spectral inverse problems: oblate lifting without prolate improvement}
\author{Jonathan Mace\\Microsoft Research \and Brian R.\ Mace\\University of Auckland}
\date{}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We revisit the spectral inverse problem for fluid-loaded elastic shells in a
shorter and narrower form than the earlier ``lifting theorem'' draft.  The
supported result is not a universal statement that symmetry breaking improves
identifiability.  It is an asymmetry result.  For the five-mode flexural map
used in the Browntone companion studies, the equivalent-sphere reduction is
rank deficient, the oblate Ritz family develops a finite near-spherical floor
and then improves substantially, whereas the prolate branch remains poorly
conditioned.  Using the canonical abdominal-shell parameters, the numerical
condition number changes from
$\kappa_\mathrm{sphere}\approx\num{1.37e10}$ to
$\kappa_\mathrm{floor}\approx\num{269}$ and then to
$\kappa_\mathrm{oblate}=\num{69.4}$, while
$\kappa_\mathrm{prolate}=\numrange{561}{729}$.  The conclusion is plain: the
recoverability of geometry from spectra depends on how curvature is
redistributed, not merely on the fact that spherical symmetry has been broken.
\end{abstract}
