\title{Geometry-dependent identifiability in spectral inverse problems: oblate lifting without prolate improvement}
\author{Jonathan Mace\\Microsoft Research \and Brian R.\ Mace\\University of Auckland}
\date{}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We consider the spectral inverse problem for fluid-loaded elastic shells using
the five-mode flexural map $\mathbf{f}=[f_2,\ldots,f_6]^\mathsf{T}$.  The
equivalent-sphere reduction is rank deficient, while the corresponding
five-mode Ritz discretisation produces a positive near-spherical intercept in
the smallest singular value that is a numerical artefact rather than a physical
effect.  Away from the immediate spherical neighbourhood, the oblate branch
shows clear growth in the weakest sensitivity direction, whereas a prolate
continuation remains less informative.  For the canonical parameters, the
equivalent-sphere model gives the numerical shadow
$\kappa_\mathrm{eq}\approx\num{1.23e10}$, the Ritz sphere gives
$\kappa_\mathrm{Ritz}=244$, the canonical oblate point gives
$\kappa_\mathrm{oblate}=\num{69.4}$, and a representative prolate endpoint at
$c/a=\num{1.5}$ gives $\kappa_\mathrm{prolate}=156$.  Identifiability therefore
depends on how curvature is redistributed, not merely on the loss of spherical
symmetry.
\end{abstract}
