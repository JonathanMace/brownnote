# Declaration of Generative AI Use

## Paper 9 — Geometry-dependent identifiability in spectral inverse problems

### Recommended statement

During manuscript preparation, the authors used GitHub Copilot CLI (GitHub,
Inc.) to assist with code development for the Ritz model and Jacobian
computation, figure generation, and language editing of the manuscript draft.
All equations, derivations, numerical results, scientific interpretations, and
conclusions were reviewed and verified by the human authors, who take full
responsibility for the content of the manuscript. No generative AI tool was
used to create or alter original research data or figures.

### Placement note

Elsevier (JSV) requires AI disclosure to be placed at the end of the
manuscript, before the references, under a dedicated "Declaration of
Generative AI and AI-assisted Technologies in the Writing Process" heading.
See: https://www.elsevier.com/about/policies-and-standards/the-use-of-generative-ai-and-ai-assisted-technologies-in-writing

### Policy summary for this package

- **Code drafting:** GitHub Copilot assisted with implementation of the
  Ritz model, scaled Jacobian computation, and singular value analysis in
  `src/analytical/kac_identifiability.py`.
- **Figure generation:** Copilot assisted with matplotlib scripts for the
  lifting theorem figure (`fig_lifting_theorem.pdf`).
- **Language editing:** Copilot suggested phrasing improvements during
  drafting of all sections.
- **Scientific content:** All physical reasoning, mathematical derivations,
  parameter choices, and interpretations are the authors' own work.
- **Data integrity:** All computational results were independently validated
  against analytical benchmarks and symmetry arguments.
