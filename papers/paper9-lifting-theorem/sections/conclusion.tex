\section{Conclusion}

Within the five-mode spectral inverse problem for a fluid-filled shell, the
equivalent-sphere reduction is singular in exact arithmetic and appears
numerically as $\kappa_\mathrm{eq}\approx\num{1.23e10}$.  The corresponding
five-mode Ritz discretisation at $a=c$ gives a finite
$\kappa_\mathrm{Ritz}=244$, but the associated positive intercept in
$\sigma_{\min}$ is a discretisation artefact, not a physical spherical lifting.
Away from the immediate spherical neighbourhood, the oblate branch increases
$\sigma_{\min}$ and reaches $\kappa_\mathrm{oblate}=\num{69.4}$ at the
canonical geometry, whereas a representative prolate endpoint at
$c/a=\num{1.5}$ gives $\kappa_\mathrm{prolate}=156$.

The conclusion is therefore specific rather than universal: identifiability in
this spectral inverse problem is geometry-dependent.  Oblate curvature
redistribution lifts the weak inverse direction; prolate distortion does not
deliver the same improvement.
