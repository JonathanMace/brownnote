# Graphical abstract concept

Create a single clean publication-quality panel with a left-to-right narrative.

## Left panel: analytical model

- Show the abdomen as a fluid-filled oblate spheroidal shell in section view.
- Label the semi-major axis, semi-minor axis, wall thickness, and fluid core.
- Overlay a representative low-order flexural mode shape (`n = 2`) using subtle
  deformation contours or outline offsets.
- Include a small inset indicating the breathing mode is far above the infrasonic
  band, while the flexural family lies near `4-10 Hz`.

## Centre panel: coupling disparity

- Place two large horizontal arrows aimed at the same resonant shell.
- The airborne pathway should be thin, cool-coloured, and labelled
  `Airborne infrasound` with `0.028 μm at 120 dB`.
- The whole-body vibration pathway should be bold, warm-coloured, and labelled
  `Whole-body vibration` with `~33,000× larger response`.
- Put the main quantitative message in the centre:
  `Mechanical coupling >> airborne coupling`.

## Right panel: physical interpretation

- Show a simple stick-figure or schematic human in two excitation scenarios:
  airborne sound on the left and seated/standing whole-body vibration on the right.
- Use a loudspeaker icon for the airborne case and a vibrating platform/seat icon
  for the WBV case.
- Visually emphasise negligible abdominal motion for airborne sound and strong
  resonant transmission for WBV.

## Style notes

- Use a restrained Elsevier/JSV-friendly palette: blues for airborne acoustics,
  orange/red for mechanical vibration, neutral grey for anatomy.
- Keep text minimal so the image remains legible at submission-thumbnail size.
- Prefer vector-style line art, smooth arrows, and one bold numerical callout:
  `33,000×`.
- The overall message should be immediately readable without humour, but the
  juxtaposition should retain a faint sense that the paper knows exactly what
  question it is answering.
