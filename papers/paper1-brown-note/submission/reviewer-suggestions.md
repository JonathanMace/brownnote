# Suggested reviewers for Paper 1

Potential reviewers were selected for expertise spanning shell vibration,
fluid--structure interaction, acoustic scattering, and whole-body vibration.
All are outside Microsoft Research and the University of Auckland.

## 1. Marco Amabili

- **Affiliation:** Department of Mechanical Engineering, McGill University
- **Email:** `marco.amabili@mcgill.ca`
- **Relevant papers:**
  - *Theory and experiments for large-amplitude vibrations of empty and fluid-filled circular cylindrical shells with imperfections*, **Journal of Sound and Vibration** 262 (2003) 921--975.
  - *Non-linear dynamics and stability of circular cylindrical shells containing flowing fluid. Part 1: Stability*, **Journal of Sound and Vibration** 225 (1999) 655--699.
- **Reason:** Amabili is one of the clearest fits for the manuscript's analytical core: nonlinear shell vibration, fluid loading, and experimentally informed mechanics. He is likely to assess the spheroidal-shell approximation and modal arguments on their technical merits.
- **Sources:**
  - McGill / research overview: <https://mechvibfluidint.research.mcgill.ca/>
  - Email confirmation and biography: <https://www.chairs-chaires.gc.ca/chairholders-titulaires/profile-eng.aspx?profileId=2595>
  - Publication list: <https://www.mcgill.ca/mecheng/files/mecheng/Biblio_amabili.pdf>

## 2. Subhash Rakheja

- **Affiliation:** Department of Mechanical, Industrial and Aerospace Engineering, Concordia University
- **Email:** `subhash.rakheja@concordia.ca`

## 3. Philip L. Marston

- **Affiliation:** Department of Physics and Astronomy, Washington State University
- **Email:** `marston@wsu.edu`
- **Relevant papers:**
  - *Acoustic scattering from fluid-filled shells: weak coupling and resonance effects*, **Journal of the Acoustical Society of America** 81 (1987) 1491--1498.
  - Selected later work on acoustic scattering and guided waves in elastic shells, relevant to long-wavelength shell-coupling physics.
- **Reason:** Marston brings exactly the kind of scattering-theory expertise that underpins the paper's Rayleigh-regime airborne-coupling argument. He is a particularly good fit for judging whether the acoustic side of the mechanistic explanation is both conservative and physically sound.
- **Sources:**
  - WSU faculty profile: <https://physics.wsu.edu/faculty-staff/wsu-profile/marston/>
  - Example shell-scattering source: <https://www.ica2013montreal.org/Proceedings/mss/055073_1.pdf>
