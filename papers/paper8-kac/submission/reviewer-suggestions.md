# Suggested reviewers for Paper 8

Suggested reviewers were chosen to cover inverse problems, structural dynamics,
shell vibration, and modal identification. These are editorial suggestions
only; the authors should still screen for conflicts of interest, recent
collaborations, and other exclusions before submission.

## 1. Marco Amabili
- **Affiliation:** Department of Mechanical Engineering, McGill University
- **Expertise:** Shell vibration, fluid--structure interaction, nonlinear shell mechanics
- **Why a good fit:** Strong match to the paper's oblate-shell modelling, modal mechanics, and curvature-driven interpretation of identifiability.

## 2. Michael I. Friswell
- **Affiliation:** College of Engineering, Swansea University
- **Expertise:** Inverse problems in structural dynamics, model updating, parameter identification
- **Why a good fit:** Particularly well placed to assess the paper's forward--inverse adequacy gap and its implications for engineering model calibration.

## 3. Michael S. Allen
- **Affiliation:** Department of Engineering Physics, University of Wisconsin--Madison
- **Expertise:** Modal analysis, system identification, nonlinear parameter estimation
- **Why a good fit:** A strong reviewer for the practical-identifiability and resonance-inversion aspects of the manuscript.

## 4. Pierre Cawley
- **Affiliation:** Department of Engineering, University of Cambridge
- **Expertise:** Inverse methods, wave-based diagnostics, ill-posed engineering inference
- **Why a good fit:** Brings inverse-problems perspective from engineering acoustics and would be well suited to judge the conditioning arguments.

## 5. Farbod Alijani
- **Affiliation:** Department of Precision and Microsystems Engineering, TU Delft
- **Expertise:** Shell dynamics, nonlinear vibrations, curved-structure mechanics
- **Why a good fit:** Well matched to the manuscript's geometry-dependent modal arguments and shell-theory interpretation.
