\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

The ``brown note'' is a hypothetical infrasonic tone---typically cited in the
range of \SIrange{5}{9}{\hertz}---that is purported to cause involuntary loss
of bowel control by inducing resonance in the human abdominal cavity
\citep{Leventhall2007}. Despite its persistence in popular culture and
occasional references in acoustics folklore, no rigorous scientific study has
been published that either confirms or definitively refutes the hypothesis on
a mechanistic basis.

The present study aims to address this gap by performing the first systematic
computational investigation of infrasound-induced resonance in the human
abdominal cavity. Specifically, we seek to answer three questions:
%
\begin{enumerate}
  \item What are the natural frequencies of the human abdominal cavity,
    modelled as a fluid-filled elastic shell?
  \item Do any of these natural frequencies fall within the infrasound range
    (\SIrange{1}{20}{\hertz})?
  \item At physiologically plausible sound pressure levels, are the resulting
    displacement amplitudes sufficient to produce mechanical effects on
    the bowel?
\end{enumerate}
