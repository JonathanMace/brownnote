\section{Airborne vs.\ Mechanical Coupling}\label{sec:coupling}

Having established that the abdomen possesses flexural resonances squarely
in the infrasonic range, we now confront the more pressing question: can
airborne sound actually \emph{excite} them?  The short answer is sobering
for proponents of the brown note.

\subsection{Airborne Acoustic Pathway}

At \SI{4}{\hertz}, the acoustic wavelength in air exceeds \SI{85}{\metre}---
some 500 times the abdominal radius.  The abdomen, acoustically speaking,
is invisible.  Table~\ref{tab:airborne} quantifies this
invisibility using the energy-consistent reciprocity analysis developed in
\S\ref{sec:formulation}.

At \SI{120}{\decibel} SPL---the threshold of pain, and roughly the level at
which conversation becomes impossible---the energy-consistent wall
displacement is \SI{0.014}{\micro\metre}.  This falls two orders of magnitude
below the $0.5$--$\SI{2.0}{\micro\metre}$ activation range of
mechanosensitive PIEZO channels \cite{coste2010piezo1}.  Reaching the
\SI{1}{\micro\metre} threshold demands approximately \SI{158}{\decibel},
a level that would cause immediate structural damage to the ear and is
available only from explosive blasts and rocket launches.  One might
reasonably conclude that hearing loss would present a more urgent clinical
concern than any intestinal disturbance.

\subsection{Mechanical (Whole-Body Vibration) Pathway}

Mechanical vibration, by contrast, does not knock politely at the acoustic
impedance door---it kicks it open.  Vibration transmitted through the
skeleton couples directly to the abdominal wall, bypassing the $(ka)^n$
penalty entirely.  Table~\ref{tab:mechanical} tells a very different story.

At the EU action value of \SI{0.5}{\metre\per\second\squared}, the predicted
displacement exceeds the PIEZO threshold by more than three orders of
magnitude.  Unlike the airborne case, this is not a theoretical curiosity:
it is the everyday reality for operators of heavy equipment, long-haul
drivers, and helicopter pilots---populations in which gastrointestinal
complaints are, perhaps unsurprisingly, well documented
\cite{griffin1990handbook}.

\subsection{The Coupling Ratio}

The ratio of mechanical to airborne displacement defines the coupling
disparity at its starkest:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{R} = \frac{\xi_\mathrm{mech}}{\xi_\mathrm{air}}
  \approx \frac{649}{0.014} \approx 4.6 \times 10^4.
  \label{eq:coupling_ratio}
\end{equation}
This four-order-of-magnitude gap is robust to parameter uncertainties because
both pathways excite the \emph{same} modal structure---the ratio depends
primarily on $(ka)^{-n}$ and the base-excitation transfer function, neither of
which is sensitive to tissue properties.  One could double the elastic modulus,
halve the wall thickness, or relocate the subject to Jupiter, and the ratio
would scarcely budge.

\subsection{Energy Budget}

We verify self-consistency through the absorption cross-section derived from
reciprocity \cite{junger2012sound}:
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_\mathrm{abs} = \frac{(2n+1)\lambda^2}{4\pi}
  \,\frac{\Gamma_\mathrm{rad}}{\Gamma_\mathrm{total}},
\end{equation}
where $\Gamma_\mathrm{rad}$ and $\Gamma_\mathrm{total}$ are the radiation and
total damping widths, respectively.  For the $n=2$ mode in air,
$\zeta_\mathrm{rad} \approx 10^{-15}$ whereas
$\zeta_\mathrm{struct} = 0.125$, giving an absorption efficiency of order
$10^{-14}$.  The shell absorbs roughly one part in a hundred trillion of
the incident acoustic energy---a figure that puts the brown note hypothesis
on rather thin thermodynamic ice.

\subsection{Comparison with Empirical Evidence}

The model predictions align with two otherwise contradictory bodies of
evidence:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{WBV studies}: ISO~2631 documents gastrointestinal symptoms
    at $4$--$\SI{8}{\hertz}$ under mechanical vibration \cite{iso2631}.
    Our model explains \emph{why} this frequency range is problematic.
  \item \textbf{Airborne infrasound studies}: No gastrointestinal effects
    have been observed at SPL $\leq \SI{150}{\decibel}$
    \cite{Leventhall2007}.  Our model explains why they should not be.
\end{itemize}
The coupling disparity resolves this apparent contradiction: both communities
are studying the same resonance through very different ends of the telescope.
