## Quantitative findings

- At the sphere limit (`c/a = 1.0000`), the canonical `n=2` Ritz frequency is `3.049606 Hz` versus the analytical sphere result `3.353118 Hz`, i.e. a bias of `-9.052\%`.
- Near the sphere limit, the higher-mode biases are larger: `n=3` gives `4.570545 Hz` versus `5.423955 Hz` (`-15.734\%`), and `n=4` gives `6.171002 Hz` versus `7.685584 Hz` (`-19.707\%`).
- For the canonical oblate geometry (`c/a \approx 0.667`), the tabled `n=2` comparison remains `4.0 Hz` (sphere) versus `3.8 Hz` (Ritz), but this difference is now described as illustrative rather than as a precise correction.
