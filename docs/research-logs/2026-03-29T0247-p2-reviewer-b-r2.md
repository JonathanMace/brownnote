# Reviewer B — Paper 2, Round 2 (Post-Reframe)

**Manuscript:** "A Helmholtz Resonator Model for Gas-Pocket Mechanotransduction in the Human Abdomen: Theoretical Framework and Sensitivity Analysis"

**Review date:** 2026-03-29

**Context:** Round 1 verdict was REJECT on four fundamental grounds. The authors have performed a major reframe from a quantitative acoustics claim into a hypothesis/theoretical framework paper. This review assesses whether the reframe is honest, complete, and publishable.

---

## Decision: MINOR REVISION

The reframe is genuine and intellectually honest. All four fatal flaws from Round 1 have been addressed not with cosmetic disclaimers but with substantive restructuring of the argument. The paper now reads as what it always should have been: a theoretical framework that identifies a candidate mechanism, characterises its critical dependencies, and defines the experiments needed to test it. One residual overclaim and a few minor technical issues remain.

## Assessment of Round 1 Fatal Flaws

### 1. Acoustic Short-Circuit — HONESTLY PRESENTED ✓

**R1 issue:** 82.7% pressure equalisation at 7 Hz kills the mechanism for open pockets; paper didn't discuss it.

**Assessment:** The short-circuit is now featured as "the most important finding of this analysis" (line 698). The 83% equalisation and its devastating consequence are stated prominently (lines 729–736): the strongest pocket drops from 1.35 µm to 0.23 µm, below the illustrative threshold. The conclusion (lines 967–973) leads with this limitation.

I verified the numbers independently:
- Helmholtz frequency: f_H = (343/2π)√(S/VL) = 15.3 Hz ✓
- Equalisation at 7 Hz: E = 1/(1+(7/15.3)²) = 0.827 ✓
- Remaining driving fraction: 0.173 ✓
- 1.35 × 0.173 = 0.23 µm ✓

**Remaining concern (Minor — see m1 below):** The equalisation formula E = 1/(1+(f/f_H)²) is a first-order low-pass filter, not the transfer function of a Helmholtz resonator. The paper acknowledges this (lines 739–742) and notes that a true Helmholtz resonator exhibits amplification near f_H. However, the acknowledgement is incomplete: below f_H, the true Helmholtz transfer function with reasonable damping (ζ = 0.5) gives H ≈ 1.095 at 7 Hz, meaning ~90% equalisation (vs. the paper's 83%). The paper's ad-hoc formula UNDERESTIMATES the severity of the short circuit. Since this error makes the mechanism look BETTER than it actually is, and the paper's conclusion is already that the short circuit is devastating, the practical impact is nil — but the physics should be stated correctly.

**Verdict:** Honestly presented. No remaining language minimises or explains it away.

### 2. Boundary Condition Inconsistency — BRUTALLY HONEST ✓

**R1 issue:** Axial mode assumes open ends; short-circuit defence assumes sealed pockets. Contradictory.

**Assessment:** Lines 753–761 state, with admirable candour:

> "These are mutually contradictory assumptions applied in different parts of the analysis to obtain favourable results in each case."

Both cases are then presented as explicit bounds (sealed = upper bound, open = lower bound) with numerical values for each. Limitation #6 (lines 877–881) repeats the acknowledgement. Neither case is favoured over the other. The paper correctly identifies experimental measurement of in-vivo pocket impedance as the way to resolve this tension (§5.1).

**Verdict:** Exemplary scientific honesty. No further action needed.

### 3. PIEZO Threshold Category Error — CLEARLY DISCLAIMED ✓

**R1 issue:** Single-cell patch-clamp threshold ≠ bulk tissue wall displacement. Paper treated them as equivalent.

**Assessment:** Section 5 (lines 429–446) now carries an extensive disclaimer:

> "We emphasise that this comparison is presented to establish *order-of-magnitude plausibility*, not quantitative equivalence."

The word "illustrative" appears 12 times in the paper (abstract, figure captions, results, discussion, conclusion). The category error is explicitly restated in Limitation #4 (lines 865–872). The experimental requirements section (§5.2) proposes the tissue-level mechanotransduction measurement needed to resolve the issue.

No remaining language treats the PIEZO comparison as validation. This is honestly framed.

**Verdict:** Fixed. No further action needed.

### 4. Monte Carlo Tautological — ACKNOWLEDGED ✓

**R1 issue:** 100% exceedance fraction is tautological given input distributions; presented as confirmatory.

**Assessment:** Lines 588–589 explicitly state:

> "The value of this analysis lies not in the exceedance fraction itself—which is tautological given the assumed distributions—"

The analysis is reframed as identifying the *sensitivity* to individual parameters (lines 590–594). The critical insight — that pocket sealing is the dominant parameter, not volume — is the actual output (lines 601–603). The median displacement under short-circuit conditions (0.18 µm, well below threshold) is stated prominently.

**Verdict:** Honestly reframed. The word "tautological" in the paper itself is a strong signal of good faith.

## Remaining Issues

### Major Issues

None. The four fatal flaws are genuinely resolved.

### Minor Issues

#### m1. Equalisation Formula Physics (Lines 729, 739–742)

The equalisation parameter E = 1/[1+(f/f_H)²] is physically incorrect for a Helmholtz resonator, as the paper partially acknowledges (lines 739–742). My independent calculation:

| Damping (ζ) | True Helmholtz H(7 Hz) | Equalisation | Net ΔP/p_ext |
|-------------|------------------------|--------------|--------------|
| 0 (undamped) | 1.265 | >100% | ~26% (reversed!) |
| 0.3 | 1.195 | >100% | ~20% |
| 0.5 | 1.095 | ~96% | ~9.5% |
| 1.0 | 0.827 | 83% | 17% |
| Paper (ad hoc) | — | 83% | 17% |

The paper's formula coincidentally matches ζ = 1.0, but GI tract damping at these frequencies has never been measured. For any ζ < 1, the short circuit is MORE severe than claimed. The paper's acknowledgement on lines 739–742 should be expanded to state that the 83%/17% split is an optimistic (upper bound) estimate of the residual driving pressure.

**Impact:** Non-material to conclusions (the paper already concludes the mechanism is suppressed for open pockets). But the physics should be correct.

#### m2. "Most Physically Plausible" Overclaim (Line 985)

The conclusion states:

> "the gas-pocket transduction hypothesis remains the most physically plausible candidate mechanism for airborne-infrasound-induced GI effects"

The paper has compared ONLY two mechanisms: whole-cavity resonance and gas-pocket transduction. It has not evaluated direct vibrotactile coupling through the abdominal wall, diaphragmatic pressure transmission, thoracic-abdominal coupling, or peristaltic entrainment. "Most plausible" requires systematic elimination of alternatives that was not performed.

**Suggested fix:** "...remains more physically plausible than whole-cavity resonance as a candidate mechanism..." or "...is the most efficient of the pathways analysed..."

#### m3. Density Inconsistency Between Code Files

`gas_pocket_resonance.py` (line 56) uses ρ_fluid = 1040 kg/m³ (which is actually the wall density), while `gas_pocket_detailed.py` (line 59) correctly uses ρ_tissue = 1020 kg/m³ matching Table 1. The paper's computations appear to use the correct file (`gas_pocket_detailed.py`), so this is a housekeeping issue, but the stale file should be either updated or clearly marked as deprecated to prevent confusion.

#### m4. Helmholtz Parameters Unjustified (Lines 720–723)

The Helmholtz estimate uses V = 200 mL, S = π(5 mm)², L_eff = 5 m. None of these are cited or derived. The effective acoustic length of the GI air column (5 m) is particularly uncertain — the actual GI tract is ~8 m but includes tortuous geometry, collapsed segments, and valves. A brief sensitivity analysis (±50% on L_eff) or anatomical justification would strengthen the estimate. Changing L_eff from 5 m to 8 m shifts f_H from 15.3 Hz to 12.1 Hz, which would INCREASE equalisation at 7 Hz and make the short circuit worse.

#### m5. Previously Identified Minor Issues Still Open

From the prior round, several minor issues remain unaddressed:
- Impedance ratio "~3600:1" (actual ~3740:1 for air at STP) — line 143
- δ_wall = 0.3 needs a peer-reviewed citation — line 346
- δ_th ≈ 0.04 should be justified for infrasound (not imported from ultrasound literature) — line 342
- Table 2 would benefit from showing cylindrical pocket lengths

None are blocking, but the first two should be addressed for a journal submission.

#### m6. Sub-Micrometre Imaging Challenge Understated (Lines 943–946)

The experimental requirements section proposes imaging gas pocket wall displacement with "spatial resolution ≤ 0.5 mm to detect the predicted sub-micrometre wall displacements." Detecting sub-micrometre displacements is fundamentally a TEMPORAL resolution / phase-sensitivity challenge, not a spatial resolution one. A 0.5 mm spatial resolution cannot directly measure a 0.2–1.3 µm displacement. The text should acknowledge that the measurement requires either phase-sensitive ultrasound (as briefly noted) or interferometric techniques, and that this is at or beyond current clinical imaging capabilities.

## Positive Comments

1. **The reframe is genuine, not cosmetic.** The paper has been structurally reorganised, not just patched with disclaimers. The short-circuit section is now central rather than defensive. The sensitivity analysis is correctly identified as parameter exploration rather than validation. The title, abstract, and conclusion are internally consistent in their hypothesis framing.

2. **Line 753–761 is the most scientifically honest passage I have reviewed this year.** Explicitly calling out contradictory boundary conditions "applied in different parts of the analysis to obtain favourable results in each case" is exactly the standard I hold authors to. More papers should do this.

3. **The Experimental Requirements section (§5) adds genuine value.** The three proposed experiments are specific, feasible, and correctly prioritised. The minimum requirement for each (e.g., 15 dB attenuation through liquid plugs for the sealed-pocket assumption) provides quantitative go/no-go criteria.

4. **The code is clean, well-tested, and matches the paper exactly.** 206 tests, all passing. Every number I checked reproduced.

5. **The constrained-bubble physics is correct.** The governing equations, limiting cases (Minnaert recovery when h_w → 0), stiffness decomposition, and sub-resonant compliance argument are all standard and properly applied.

6. **The paper fills an important gap in the infrasound biology literature.** Whether or not the mechanism operates in vivo, the theoretical framework correctly identifies the key unknowns and the measurements needed to resolve them. This is how hypothesis papers should work.

---

## Summary

The reframe from quantitative acoustics claim to hypothesis/theoretical framework paper is honest and complete. All four Round 1 fatal flaws are genuinely addressed: the short-circuit is now the central finding, the boundary condition inconsistency is explicitly acknowledged, the PIEZO comparison is clearly marked as illustrative, and the sensitivity analysis tautology is stated in the paper's own text.

Two minor issues require attention: (1) the "most physically plausible" claim should be scoped to the mechanisms actually analysed, and (2) the equalisation formula physics should be corrected or the optimistic nature of the 83% figure acknowledged.

The paper is publishable as a hypothesis paper after these minor corrections. The code is verified, the numbers are internally consistent, and the scientific reasoning is sound within the clearly stated bounds.

**Bottom line:** Honest reframe. Fix the overclaim on line 985 and expand the equalisation caveat. Then this is ready for submission as a hypothesis paper to JASA or JSV.
