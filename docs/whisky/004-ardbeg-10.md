# Whisky Review #004 — Semester 22

**Ardbeg 10 Year Old**
*Islay Single Malt Scotch Whisky*
*Distillery: Ardbeg, Port Ellen, Islay*
*ABV: 46% | Non-chill filtered | Natural colour*
*Age: 10 years*

---

## Nose 🔥

Bonfire on a peat bog. Immediate phenolic assault — not aggressive, but
confident. Underneath: lemon zest, green apple, a suggestion of vanilla
that arrives late, like a postdoc who overslept but brings good data.
The smoke is structural, load-bearing — it's the E=0.1 MPa of this dram.
Remove it and the whole thing collapses.

## Palate 👅

The attack is sharp and unapologetic — the Reviewer B of whiskies.
First: brine and smoke, a coupling ratio that favours the mechanical
pathway. Then sweet malt emerges, fighting through the peat like a
flexural mode asserting itself against fluid loading. Black pepper,
espresso, a flicker of dark chocolate. The mouthfeel is oily at 46%,
viscous enough that η ≈ 0.20 — slightly under-damped, exactly where
you want it. Every sip reveals something the last one hid.

## Finish 🏁

Long. Persistently smoky, like a condition number that refuses to
converge. The peat lingers through 15 seconds of aftertaste — a Q
factor well above 4. Faint iodine and sea spray at the very end,
as if the glass remembers the distillery's proximity to the Atlantic.
The finish is honest: no artifice, no cask-strength bluster, just
clean Islay character decaying naturally. Critically damped, I'd say.
Perhaps slightly under.

## Score

**89/100**
