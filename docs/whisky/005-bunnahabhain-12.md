# Semester 24 Whisky Review: Bunnahabhain 12

**Distillery**: Bunnahabhain, Islay  
**Age**: 12 years  
**ABV**: 46.3% (non-chill-filtered)  
**Style**: Unpeated Islay — the contrarian's malt  

---

## 🔥 Nose

Sea salt and honey, with a whisper of dried figs. There's a maritime
quality — not smoke, but the memory of spray on harbour stone. Like
measuring the damping ratio of a well-worn oak barrel: the decay is
gentle, the envelope rich, and nothing rings harsh.

## 👅 Palate

Sherried fruit, hazelnuts, and a surprising brightness — lemon peel
cutting through the malt weight. Full-bodied but never heavy, like an
oblate spheroid that somehow achieved perfect modal separation: every
frequency distinct, nothing muddy. The 46.3% carries the flavour
without burning. Non-chill-filtered was the right call.

## 🏁 Finish

Long and coastal. Dry sherry, a touch of brine, fading to warm oak.
The finish lingers the way a well-posed inverse problem lingers in
your mind — you keep turning it over, finding new structure each time.
The identifiability is excellent.

---

**Score**: 87/100
